Minimum Equipment Required for Flight.

MINIMUM EQUIPMENT REQUIRED FOR FLIGHT, Section 91.205.

For VFR day, the mnemonic "TOMATO FLAMES":
- T - Tachometer for each engine.
- O - Oil pressure gauge for each engine.
- M - Manifold pressure gauge for each altitude engine.
- A - Altimeter.
- T - Temperature gauge for each liquid-cooled engine.
- O - Oil temperature indicator for each engine.
- F - Fuel quantity gauge for each tank.
- L - Landing gear position lights, if retractable gear.
- A - Anticollision lights, for aircraft certified after March 11, 1996.
- M - Magnetic direction indicator, the magnetic compass.
- E - ELT, if required by Section 91.207.
- S - Safety belt and shoulder harness.

For VFR night: all VFR day equipment, plus the mnemonic "FLAPS":
- F - Fuses, a spare set.
- L - Landing light, if operating for hire.
- A - Anticollision lights.
- P - Position lights, the navigation lights.
- S - Source of electrical power, such as a battery.

For IFR day: all VFR day equipment, plus the mnemonic "GRAB CARD":
- G - Generator or alternator.
- R - Radios: two-way radio communication and navigational equipment suitable for the route to be flown.
- A - Altimeter, sensitive and adjustable for barometric pressure.
- B - Ball, the slip-skid indicator.
- C - Clock, showing hours, minutes and seconds, with a sweep-second pointer or digital representation, installed as part of the aircraft equipment.
- A - Attitude indicator.
- R - Rate-of-turn indicator.
- D - Directional gyro, the heading indicator.

For IFR night: all VFR day equipment, plus VFR night equipment, plus GRAB CARD.

Flight at and above Flight Level 240: when using VOR for navigation, DME or RNAV is also required.

If operating for hire over water and beyond power-off gliding distance from shore: an approved flotation device is required for each occupant, and at least one pyrotechnic signaling device.

OPERATING WITH INOPERATIVE ITEMS, Section 91.213. If the aircraft has an approved Minimum Equipment List, or MEL, follow the MEL's provisions.

If the aircraft does not have an MEL, ask whether the inoperative equipment is required by any of the following: VFR-day type certification requirements; the equipment list or kind-of-operations equipment list; Section 91.205 or other regulations for the kind of operation being conducted; or an Airworthiness Directive.

If the answer is yes to any of those, flying is not allowed without a special flight permit, unless operating under Part 121.

If the answer is no to all of those, flying is permitted, provided: the inoperative equipment is removed, if it affects weight and balance, or is deactivated and placarded "Inoperative"; and the pilot or a mechanic determines the inoperative item creates no hazard.
