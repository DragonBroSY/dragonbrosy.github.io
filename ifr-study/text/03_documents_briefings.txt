Personal Documents, Aircraft Documents, and Briefings.

PERSONAL DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR FLIGHT
- Pilot Certificate.
- Medical certificate, or US Driver's license as permitted by Section 61.113 and Section 61.23.
- Authorized photo ID, such as a passport or driver's license.
- Restricted Radiotelephone Operator Permit, for flights outside the US.

AIRCRAFT DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR FLIGHT, the mnemonic "ARROW":
- A - Airworthiness certificate.
- R - Registration certificate.
- R - Radio station license, for flights outside the US.
- O - Operating limitations and information, found in the AFM.
- W - Weight and Balance data, aircraft specific.
References: Section 21.5, Section 91.103, Section 91.9, Section 91.203, ICAO Article 29.

AIRCRAFT MAINTENANCE INSPECTIONS REQUIRED FOR IFR, the mnemonic "AVIATES":
- A - Airworthiness Directive, or AD, required inspections. Section 39.
- V - VOR check every 30 days, for IFR. Section 91.171.
- I - Inspections, under Section 91.409:
  - Annual inspection, every 12 calendar months, for all aircraft.
  - 100-hour, time-in-service, inspection required if carrying a person for hire, other than a crew member, or flight instructing for hire in an aircraft provided by the person giving the instruction. "For hire" refers to the person, not the aircraft: a flight school providing an airplane plus instructor for hire requires the 100-hour inspection; a student-owned aircraft does not; a rental with no pilot or instructor does not.
  - The 100-hour inspection may be exceeded by up to 10 hours if the aircraft is en route to a place where the inspection can be done. This additional time must be included when computing the next 100-hour inspection.
  - An annual inspection can substitute for the 100-hour if done within 100 hours of time-in-service.
  - A progressive inspection schedule, if specifically approved by the FAA, may replace the annual and 100-hour inspections.
- A - Altimeter, automatic altitude reporting used by the transponder, and static system, every 24 calendar months, for IFR in controlled airspace. Section 91.411.
- T - Transponder, every 24 calendar months. Section 91.413.
- E - ELT, Section 91.207, inspected every 12 calendar months. Battery must be replaced after more than 1 hour of cumulative transmitter use, or if 50% of its useful life has expired, or for rechargeable batteries, 50% of the useful life of charge has expired.
- S - Supplemental Type Certificate, or STC, required inspections.
Reference: Pilot's Handbook of Aeronautical Knowledge.

BRIEFINGS

PASSENGER BRIEFING, the mnemonic "SAFETY":
- S - Seat belts fastened for taxi, takeoff, and landing. Shoulder harness fastened for takeoff and landing. Seat position adjusted and locked in place.
- A - Air vents location and operation. All environmental controls discussed. Action in case of any passenger discomfort.
- F - Fire extinguisher location and operation.
- E - Exit doors: how to secure, how to open. Emergency evacuation plan. Emergency and survival kit location and contents.
- T - Traffic: scanning, spotting, and notifying the pilot. Talking, and sterile flight deck expectations.
- Y - Your questions? Speak up!
Reference: Pilot's Handbook of Aeronautical Knowledge.

TAXI BRIEFING, the mnemonic "ARCH":
- A - Assigned or planned runway.
- R - Route.
- C - Crossings and hold short instructions.
- H - Hot spots and Hazards, such as NOTAMs, closed taxiways or runways, and surface condition.

TAKEOFF BRIEFING, the mnemonic "DEPARTS":
- D - Departure review, such as takeoff type, initial heading, first fix and course, and clearance readout.
- E - Establish Expectations, such as who is the flying pilot, who is PIC, and positive transfer of controls.
- P - Plan and special considerations, such as weather, visibility, terrain, unfamiliar field, and inoperative equipment or MELs.
- A - Alternate: takeoff alternate if needed, or return plan.
- R - Runway conditions and length.
- T - Trouble or Tactics, such as rejected takeoff or engine failure.
- S - Speak up! Questions or concerns?
